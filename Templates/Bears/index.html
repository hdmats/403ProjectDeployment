Welcome to the Yellowstone Bear Tracker!

This website provides a venue for all the bear related info you need for your trip at Yellowstone. We provided an open database to allow users to view bear sightings and details, as well as enter in and update your very own encounters! Also, we have a page on bear safety so you can be educated and prepared in the event you encounter a bear. We hope you enjoy your trip. Happy bear finding!

There are over 500 bears in the Greater Yellowstone Ecosystem

Bears have a high IQ- They have the largest and most complex brain compared to other land mammals their size. Grizzly bears can remember valuable spots for food after multiple years, and have even been observed to cover their tracks and hide behind rocks or trees to avoid detection by hunters.

Bears are fiercely protective of their family members and will go to great lengths to keep them safe. This often means putting their own lives on the line, as bears will fight aggressively to defend their young from threats. In fact, some bears have been known to fight to the death in order to protect their cubs or siblings. This strong sense of family and loyalty is just one of the many reasons why bears are such amazing animals.

Bear sightings are often more common in urban or suburban areas than in wilderness areas. This is because bears are opportunistic feeders and will take advantage of any available food source, including garbage, bird feeders, and even pet food left outside. As a result, bears will often venture into populated areas in search of a meal, leading to more frequent sightings. In fact, some bear populations are so used to human presence that they have become accustomed to people and will even approach them in search of food. This can be dangerous for both the bears and humans, so it is important to take steps to prevent bears from accessing human food sources.
