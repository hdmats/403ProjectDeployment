Stay Safe Around Bears

  1. Be aware of your surroundings and avoid surprising bears. Make noise, sing, clap, or talk loudly when you are hiking in bear country to let bears know you are there.
  2. Keep a safe distance from bears. Never approach a bear, especially if it is a mother with cubs. If you see a bear, avoid direct eye contact, as this can be seen as a threat.
  3. Do not feed bears. Bears that become accustomed to human food can become aggressive and dangerous. Keep a clean campsite and store all food, trash, and other scented items in bear-proof containers or locked in your vehicle.
  4. Carry bear spray and know how to use it. Bear spray is a non-lethal way to deter bears if they charge or attack. Keep it readily accessible and know how to use it properly.
  5. If you see a bear, stay calm and do not run. Running can trigger a bear's predatory instincts and may cause it to chase you. Instead, slowly back away and give the bear plenty of space to pass.

To safely bear watch, follow these guidelines:

  1. Choose a safe location. Avoid areas where bears are known to frequent, such as berry patches or streams where they may be fishing. Look for areas with good visibility and a safe distance from the bears.
  2. Use binoculars or a spotting scope to watch bears from a distance. This will allow you to see the bears without disturbing them or putting yourself in danger.
